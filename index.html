About This Groundbreaking Sales Book:

"The New Strategic Selling" is more than just a book; it's a sales revolution. First published in 1985, this timeless classic redefined the sales landscape by moving beyond outdated manipulative tactics and introducing a powerful, process-driven methodology. It was here that the globally impactful concept of "Win-Win" was first championed, fundamentally altering how businesses approach client relationships and B2B sales.

This meticulously updated edition brings the core principles into the modern era, directly confronting the complexities of contemporary business-to-business sales. It's packed with new, relevant real-world examples, cutting-edge strategies for navigating fierce competition, and a dedicated section addressing the most frequently asked questions from exclusive Miller Heiman workshops. This makes it an essential guide for mastering strategic selling.

Co-authored by Stephen E. Heiman and Diane Sanchez with Tad Tuleja, and featuring a compelling foreword by J.W. Marriott, Jr., this book is an indispensable resource for any sales professional, sales manager, or business leader committed to achieving sustained and unparalleled success in today's competitive market.

What You Will Master in "The New Strategic Selling":

  • Identify Key Decision Makers: Learn to pinpoint the four crucial decision makers within any corporate structure for effective strategic selling.
  • Neutralize Deal-Killers: Discover proven strategies to prevent internal sabotage that can derail your sales deals.
  • Engage Senior Executives: Master the art of making high-level executives eager to meet with you, opening doors to new opportunities.
  • Strategic Deal Closure: Avoid closing business that ultimately leads to regret, focusing on profitable and sustainable partnerships.
  • Sustainable Territory Management: Implement techniques for consistent, predictable revenue, eliminating "boom and bust" cycles in your sales territory.
  • Outsmart Competition: Understand and avoid the single most common error when facing competitors, gaining a distinct advantage.
